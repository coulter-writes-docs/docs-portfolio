# WorkHop user personas

The descriptions below outline the 4 main WorkHop user personas. Read about their unique goals, needs, technical expertise, and common WorkHop use cases.


## Dana - Senior leader

Dana is a director, VP, or departmental lead. She does not answer surveys or directly engage with colleagues in the platform. Dana uses WorkHop's analytics and reporting features to spot engagement trends from her organization.

**Goals:**

- Maintain high-level awareness of engagement and well-being trends across the organisation
- Find topics to discuss with the managers who report to her (like [Marcia](#marcia-team-manager))
- Use engagement data to inform strategic decisions about culture and retention

**Needs:**

- Clear dashboards that surface trends without extensive navigation
- Confidence that the data she sees is anonymised and aggregated appropriately


**Technical expertise:**

Low. Dana expects WorkHop to surface relevant information with minimal effort. She does not configure anything or navigate complex workflows.

**Example use cases:**

- Reviewing a monthly engagement summary for her department ahead of a leadership meeting
- Keeping record of topics she discusses with the managers who report to her
- Comparing engagement scores against previous periods to assess the impact of large projects or organisational changes

---

## Henry - HR administrator

Henry is an HR generalist. He is the primary WorkHop administrator for his organisation. He configures survey cycles, manages users and teams, manages user attributes for trend analysis, oversees AI course suggestion settings, and monitors engagement data.

**Goals:**

- Keep a pulse on org-wide employee engagement
- Set up survey cycles that generate reliable, actionable data
- Make sure the platform is set up so employees (like [Eli](#eli-employee)) have seamless access to WorkHop

**Needs:**

- Flexible survey creation and scheduling
- Simple user and team management workflows
- Admin oversight of AI course suggestions

**Technical expertise:**

Moderate. Henry is an experienced user of HRIS platforms and expects to spend time setting up and maintaining WorkHop. He has some understanding of cross-platform API integrations.

**Use cases:**

- Launching a monthly employee satisfaction survey
- Inviting a new cohort of employees and assigning them to the correct teams
- Creating a *Location* attribute, then filtering reports by employee location
- Reviewing AI course suggestion activity
- Pulling engagement reports to share with Dana ahead of a leadership review

---

## Marcia - Team manager

Marcia manages a team of 6 employees. She uses WorkHop to review anonymous feedback from her team, schedule one-on-ones, and suggest growth opportunities using AI-generated course suggestions.


**Goals:**

- Catch team issues early so she can action solutions
- Support her team's growth with AI course suggestions
- Maintain open communication with each team member

**Needs:**

- Aggregated survey answers that do not reveal respondent identity
- AI course suggestions relevant to each employee's role and growth stage
- A reliable way to schedule and track one-on-one meetings

**Technical expertise:**

Moderate to low. Marcia is comfortable with common workplace tools. She does not manage settings but can view reports and survey responses from her team.

**Use cases:**

- Viewing her team's aggregated survey responses
- Using AI course suggestions to build a case to promote one of her team members to a new role
- Scheduling one-on-ones with each direct report to address issues surfaced through WorkHop surveys

---

## Eli - Employee

Eli is an individual contributor. They use WorkHop primarily to complete surveys and schedule check-ins with their manager. Eli does not set up any part of WorkHop or view survey responses from other employees.

**Goals:**

- Share honest feedback about their work experience without fear of identification
- Maintain a regular cadence of check-ins with their manager

**Needs:**

- A simple interface for completing surveys
- Confidence that their responses are anonymous
- An easy way to schedule and track conversations with their manager

**Technical expertise:**

Low to moderate. Eli is comfortable with standard workplace tools. Once added to WorkHop, they can respond to surveys from the medium of their choice (email, text, or WorkHop). 

**Use cases:**

- Filling out a quarterly engagement survey from Henry
- Requesting a one-on-one meeting with Marcia through WorkHop
